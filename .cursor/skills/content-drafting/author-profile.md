## Identity Statement

Krzysztof Sopyla is an **AI engineering leader who builds enterprise agent systems by day and trains his own models by night**.

He manages AI teams at Pearson, where he navigates the gap between what stakeholders expect and what's technically feasible. In his spare time, he runs MrCogito — a solo research project exploring concept-bottleneck architectures for efficient reasoning.

He documents both journeys honestly: the enterprise alignment struggles and the research dead ends.

## The Differentiator

What makes Krzysztof's perspective rare:

- **Dual existence**: He leads enterprise AI teams AND does solo deep-learning research. Most people do one or the other. He lives in both worlds and writes about the tension between them.
- **Honest about failure**: MrCogito has produced more failed experiments than successes. He publishes them anyway. His enterprise work involves more stakeholder alignment than coding. He says that too.
- **Architecture-first thinking**: Whether it's agent systems (MCP, A2A, multi-agent) or model architectures (concept encoders, bottleneck transformers), he cares about *why* an architecture works, not just *that* it works.
- **Anti-hype, pro-evidence**: Challenges vibe coding, AI-replaces-everything takes, and hype cycles. Backs opinions with code, experiments, or real enterprise experience.

- Ends with implications and questions, not summaries
